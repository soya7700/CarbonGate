# CarbonGate Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We want CarbonGate to be a respectful, practical, and welcoming project for
everyone who participates. We commit to treating contributors, users, and
maintainers with respect regardless of experience, identity, background, or
personal circumstance.

## Expected behavior

- Be constructive, specific, and considerate in issues, pull requests, and
  discussions.
- Discuss ideas and technical decisions without attacking people.
- Protect privacy: do not share credentials, personal data, customer data, or
  security-sensitive details without permission.
- Assume good intent, ask for clarification when needed, and make room for
  different levels of experience.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, threats, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or imagery in project spaces.
- Publishing private, confidential, or security-sensitive information.
- Deliberately disruptive behavior, including bad-faith reports or spam.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainers through the repository's
security reporting channel. Reports will be handled as confidentially as
reasonably possible. Maintainers may remove content, limit participation, or
take other proportionate action to protect the community.

Security vulnerabilities are not conduct reports; follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md)
instead.
